Caffeine works by blocking adenosine receptors in the brain — adenosine is the neurotransmitter that builds up through the day and makes you feel tired. By blocking it, caffeine keeps you alert and energized. It also triggers adrenaline release and improves reaction time. Above 300mg, some users experience jitteriness — pairing with L-Theanine (200mg) smooths the experience significantly.

L-Theanine increases alpha brain wave activity — the same state associated with relaxed focus, like meditation. Paired with caffeine in a 2:1 ratio (200mg theanine : 100mg caffeine), it creates "clean energy" — alert and sharp, without jitteriness or a crash. One of the most synergistic combinations in sports nutrition.

L-Citrulline bypasses liver metabolism and converts directly to L-Arginine in the kidneys — far more effective than supplementing arginine directly. More arginine = more nitric oxide = vasodilation = bigger pumps, better endurance, faster recovery. Look for at least 6g per serving — anything less is underdosed.

Over 500 peer-reviewed studies make creatine monohydrate the single most researched supplement in sports nutrition. Effects build over 2–4 weeks of daily use. Monohydrate remains the gold standard — fancier forms have no proven advantage.
